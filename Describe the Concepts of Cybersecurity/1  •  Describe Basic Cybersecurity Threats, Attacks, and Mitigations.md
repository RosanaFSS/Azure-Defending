<h3>MFA</h3>

<p>Which mitigation strategy involves requiring multiple forms of identification to verify user identity and prevent unauthorized access?<br>
<code>Multifactor authentication</code></p>

<h3>Ransomware</h3>

<p>A user reports their system being locked with a demand for payment to regain access. What type of malware has likely infected the system?<br>
<code>Ransomware</code></p>

<h3>Trojan</h3>

<p>Your organization discovers an unauthorized application that seems legitimate but is secretly performing malicious activities.<br> 
What type of malware is this likely to be?<br> 
<code>Trojan</code></p>

<p>Which characteristic uniquely identifies a trojan compared to other types of malware such as viruses and worms?<br> 
<code>It masquerades as legitimate software to deceive users.</code></p>

<h3>Spyware</h3>

<p>What distinguishes spyware from other types of malware?<br>
<code>It secretly monitors user activity and collects information.</code></p>

<p>After installing a seemingly helpful app, your device starts sending information to unknown servers.<br>
What type of malware might this app contain?<br>
<code>Spyware</code></p>

<h3>Botnet</h3>

<p>What is the primary goal of a botnet payload in malware?<br>
<code>To connect a device to a network for remote control</code></p>

<p>A company discovers that one of its computer systems is part of a botnet used to mine cryptocurrencies.<br>
What type of malware payload is responsible for this situation?<br>
<code>Botnet payload</code></p>

<p>Your organization discovers multiple devices acting unusually slow, likely participating in unauthorized crypto-mining.<br>
What type of malware is likely responsible?<br>
<code>Botnet</code></p>

<h3>Worm</h3>

<p>How does a worm differ from a virus in terms of propagation across systems?
A worm spreads without needing user action, while a virus requires user action to propagate.
```

<h3>Backdoor</h3>

<p>Which type of malware payload is designed to secretly provide unauthorized access by bypassing security measures?<br>
<code>Backdoor</code></p>

<h3>Brute Force Attack</h3>

<p>What distinguishes a brute force attack from other types of password attacks?<br>
<code>It involves trying numerous password combinations until the correct one is found.</code></p>

<h3>Wireless Attack</h3>

<p>Which attack vector is used by cybercriminals to exploit unsecured wireless networks at public locations?<br>
<code>Wireless networks</code></p>
