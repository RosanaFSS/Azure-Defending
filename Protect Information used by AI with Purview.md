<strong>Microsoft Purview</strong> helps reduce these risks by applying consistent protections across AI interactions.</p>

<h3>Understand AI Data Security Risks</h3>
<p>AI tools introduce new security and compliance challenges. Without proper visibility and enforcement, organizations <code>risk</code>:<br>
  
- Data Exposure.<br>
- Compliance Violations.<br>
- Security Gaps.<br>

Managing AI-related risks requires:<br>

- <code>Visibility</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; into how orgaization data is accessed and used during AI interactions.<br>
- <code>Protections</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; for sensitive content.<br>
- <code>Policies</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; that prevent unauthorized sharing.</p>

<br>
<h3>4 Main AI Security Risks<br><br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 1 ·  Limited Visibility into AI usage</h3>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Understanding the <code>type of AI tool</code> helps determine <code>what protections</code> are available and <code>how to configure</code> them.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  To apply protections or to assess risks is necessary <code>visibility</code>, knowing:</p>

>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>Who</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; is using AI tools.<br>
>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>What</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; data is being shared.<br>
>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>How</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; AI-generated content is used and stored.<br>

<br>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<code>Three</code> categories of AI tools:</p>

> A ·  <code>Copilot experiences</code><br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Microsoft-managed Copilots integrated into Microsoft 365, such as Microsoft 365 Copilot,<br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Security Copilot,<br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Copilot Studio,<br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Copilot in Fabric.<br>

> B ·  <code>Enterprise AI apps</code><br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Organizationally connected AI apps like ChatGPT Enterprise or Azure AI services that<br>might integrate through Microsoft Entra data connectors, or Azure AI Foundry.<br>

> C ·  <code>Other AI apps</code><br>
>> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Browser-based generative AI tools like ChatGPT (consumer) and Gemini,<br>which operate outside the Microsoft 365 environment.<br>

<h3>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 2 · Data Exposure in AI Interactions</h3>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;  Organizations need controls in place to prevent these exposures before they happen.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   AI interactions can lead to unintentional data exposure through:</p>

> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Sensitive content being entered into prompts.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; AI-generated responses including confidential data.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Summarization or referencing of overshared or unprotected content.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Access to files shared too broadly, such as anonymous links or large group permissions.<br>

<h3>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 3 · Compliance and Regulatory Risks</h3>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; AI-generated content might include:</p>

> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Regulated data that isn't properly controlled.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Information shared with external tools that bypass compliance safeguards.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Activity that lacks audit trails, complicating reporting<br>

<h3>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 4 · Risks from AI-generated content</h3>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Without oversight, AI-generated content can create new security and compliance issues.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Beyond analyzing data, AI tools produce content that might introduce new risks, such as:</p>

> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Generation of confidential or sensitive information.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Creation of inappropriate or noncompliant text.<br>
> ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; Storage of content outside protected systems.<br>

<br>
<h3>Addressing Security Gaps in the AI Age<br></h3>
<p>AI interactions operate differently than traditional file sharing or communication.<br>Organizations need to:</p>

>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>Who</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Apply policies that prevent unauthorized access or misuse.<br>
>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>What</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Track what data is shared or generated<br>
>  ▪  &nbsp;&nbsp; <code>How</code> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Identify which AI tools are in use and how they interact with organizational data.<br>
